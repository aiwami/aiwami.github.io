AIWA-Michigan is a group of Detroit-area South Asian Women who seek to promote awareness of issues relevant to ourselves and our community. Through seminars and activities we are increasing the knowledge of health, financial, and retirement planning in our community. We also provide resources for families in crisis, a women’s support network, and also leisure activities for Indian women to enjoy ourselves and meet others with similar interests.

Our Mission Statement:

The Asian Indian Women’s Association provides a forum for dialogue and discussion on issues in the field of education, social welfare, and culture.

Our Vision:

To increase the knowledge of issues related to women.

To sponsor programs that provide a forum for dialogue and discussion on issues of interest to Indian women in the fields of education, social welfare, culture, and business.

To focus on what we can do to strengthen our community, including our youth, children, friends, neighbors, and ourselves.

To increase the visibility of Indian women and to promote their equal role in the community.

Our Background:

We are a group of South Asian Women who seek to promote an awareness of issues relevant to our selves and our community. Through various seminars and activities we are increasing the knowledge of health, financial and retirement planning (covering retirement housing, long-term care and Social Security) in the community.

We also provide resources for family crisis, a women’s support network, and also leisure activities primarily for Indian women to relax and meet others with similar interests. Since its inception in January 2000, the Asian Indian Women’s Association has been growing steadily. In the last twelve years, we have organized several seminars and leisure activities.
